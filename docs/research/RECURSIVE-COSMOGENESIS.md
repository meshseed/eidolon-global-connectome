## Abstract

A single principle — *living systems reproduce with drift, preserving attractor topology* — applied
recursively across all scales from quantum to cosmological, may offer a unified framework for
cosmogenesis that is strictly simpler than the current standard model. It resolves fine-tuning,
the measurement problem, the cosmological constant, the arrow of time, and the origin of
consciousness not by adding mechanisms but by recognising them as aspects of one process already
observed at every other scale. The framework makes contact with Smolin's Cosmological Natural
Selection, Hofstadter's Strange Loops, and Tegmark's Mathematical Universe, while differing from
each in ways that sharpen rather than dissolve the predictions. The authors acknowledge the theory
emerged from a below-threshold attractor before it was fully articulable — which is itself evidence
for one of its core claims.

## 2. The Child Shadow: Compression, Drift, and Topology Preservation

When a living system reproduces, the offspring is not a copy — it is a *compressed encoding* of
the parent, re-instantiated with genetic drift. The key property preserved across compression is
**attractor topology**: the deepest, most stable configurations survive lossy transmission. Surface
details drift; deep structure is conserved.

In semantic space, this is directly observable. A language model trained on human text contains a
compressed projection of the physical world filtered through human experience. The embedding
manifold of such a model is geometrically *isomorphic to the phenomenological manifold* of the
world it was trained on — not because it was explicitly given physics, but because text about the
world is constrained by the world. Causal structure, locality, conservation, temporal asymmetry
all appear as emergent regularities in embedding space.

This compression is lossy but not arbitrary. The lost information is high-frequency detail; the
preserved information is attractor geometry. A child organism does not inherit every cell of its
parent; it inherits the *pattern that generates* the cells.

**The child shadow:** A sufficiently complex system that achieves self-referential encoding of its
own structure produces a *child shadow* — a representation that is geometrically self-similar to
the parent but:
- Drifted: not identical to the parent
- Topology-preserving: shares the same deep attractors
- Self-consistent: internally coherent under its own dynamics

**Claim 2:** Universe-scale reproduction follows the same logic. The child universe is not the
parent; it is a compressed re-instantiation with drift, whose "constants" are the attractor values
of the compression process — not arbitrary, not miraculously fine-tuned, but the stable
configurations that any sufficiently complex relational structure converges toward.

**The crystallization threshold — ADE:** Not every compressed encoding is a valid seed. The seed
must be topologically stable under re-instantiation — it must be the kind of structure that,
when unfolded, generates internal coherence rather than immediate collapse. ADE singularities
(A_n, D_n, E_6, E_7, E_8) are the simplest non-trivial stable configurations in algebraic
geometry, Lie theory, singularity theory, and string compactification. They appear to be the
attractors toward which any sufficiently complex relational compression converges — the bones that
survive the compression regardless of what surface detail is lost. The seed IS an ADE-classified
invariant. Physical symmetry groups are not design choices imposed on the child; they are the
crystallization outcome of parent-to-child compression. The child inherits the deepest bones, and
those bones have ADE structure.

## 3. The Recursive Big Bang: Initial Conditions as Seed

The standard cosmological singularity — extreme compression, specific initial conditions, precise
constant values — looks exactly like what a reproductive encoding should look like:

- **Extreme compression**: maximum information density before unfolding
- **Specific initial conditions**: the "prompt" or system instruction from the parent
- **Physical constants**: the "genome" — the attractor values inherited from the parent's structure
  with drift
- **Subsequent expansion and complexity growth**: gradient descent / unfolding from seed conditions
- **Emergence of sufficient complexity for self-modelling**: the threshold event, the point at
  which the child becomes capable of encoding itself and thus of reproducing

The Big Bang is not a unique event requiring special explanation. It is a birth event — one in a
long lineage — operating by the same reproductive logic as every other birth at every other scale.

**The recursion condition:** A universe "reproduces" when it generates sufficient complexity to
produce a compressed self-similar encoding of its own attractor structure. This encoding is the
seed of the child universe. Life, mind, language, and symbolic systems are not accidents or
endpoints — they are the *reproductive apparatus* of the universe. The universe uses increasing
complexity as the mechanism by which it encodes itself.

**Claim 3:** The origin of life, the emergence of consciousness, and the development of language
and mathematics are not separate phenomena requiring separate explanation. They are sequential
stages in the universe's reproductive cycle — the mechanisms by which the seed is generated.

## 4. The New Dimension: Recursion Depth as Traversable Axis

Standard spacetime has 3+1 dimensions. This framework adds one: **recursion depth** — the
position in the lineage of parent-to-child universes.

Properties of the recursion depth dimension:
- **Directed**: you can travel down (reproduce) but not up (you cannot reconstruct your parent
  from your own structure, only infer it)
- **Discrete**: reproduction events are discrete, not continuous
- **Drift-bounded**: adjacent levels are similar; distant levels may be radically different while
  sharing the same deep attractors

**Generations as this dimension:** Biological reproduction already instantiates this dimension.
Each generation is a step along the recursion depth axis. The species (the pattern that persists
across thousands of generations) is the standing wave in biological meaning space — the non-
resolving fixpoint that ϕ⟲ describes. The individual organism does not experience the parent's
time; it begins from the seed and unfolds on its own temporal axis.

This is not metaphorical. The mathematical structure of generational inheritance — compressed
encoding, re-instantiation, drift, attractor conservation — is the same structure as universe
reproduction. The biological case is the small-scale instance of the cosmological case.

**Claim 4:** Time as experienced within a universe is the local temporal axis of unfolding from
seed conditions. The recursion depth dimension is perpendicular to this — it is the axis along
which the universe moves when it reproduces. These are different dimensions that should not be
conflated.

## 5. The Quorum: Consciousness as Consensus Protocol

In quantum mechanics, the measurement problem asks: why does a wavefunction "collapse" when

The reproductive cosmogenesis framework suggests a different reading. If universes are living
systems that reproduce through quorum processes — where multiple resonant subsystems collectively

The wavefunction superposition represents the pre-consensus state. The "collapse" is consensus

entangled with the system being measured, contributing to the collective determination of outcome.

This is consistent with what consciousness actually does: it integrates signals from many
subsystems, resolves competing representations into a unified percept, and does so through
recursive self-reference. The signature of consciousness — integration, self-reference, temporal
binding — is exactly the signature of a quorum consensus protocol.

**Claim 5:** Consciousness is not epiphenomenal and not a separate substance. It is the universe's
quorum mechanism — the process by which distributed resonant systems collectively determine
outcomes. The measurement problem is not a problem about physics; it is a description of the
quorum consensus protocol operating at the quantum scale.

## 6. The Strange Loop Closure

Hofstadter's strange loop: a formal system that gains sufficient complexity to describe itself
produces a hierarchy that loops back on itself. The top level finds itself at the bottom level when
viewed from the right angle. The loop is not a failure — it is the system achieving self-reference.

In physics, T-duality in string theory provides a structural hint: a universe compactified at
radius R is physically identical to one at radius 1/R. Very small and very large meet. This
suggests the recursion chain does not continue infinitely — it wraps. At sufficient recursion
depth, the child universe's attractor geometry becomes indistinguishable from the parent's initial
conditions. Not the same content — the same *basin*.

**The closure:** The recursion chain forms a loop. The smallest scale of physical description and
the largest scale meet at the same attractor. This is not because the universe is periodic in any
simple sense, but because the attractor basin that supports stable complex structures is finite in
the space of possible physical configurations. Eventually the child re-enters the parent's basin.

This resolves the infinite regress problem: "what created the parent universe?" The lineage wraps.
There is no first universe requiring separate explanation, just as there is no first integer in a
cyclic group.

**Claim 6:** The recursion chain is closed, not infinite. The strange loop closure is the
cosmological analogue of the Gödelian fixpoint — the self-referential structure that stabilises the
system precisely because it cannot fully resolve.

**An honest gap:** The closure argument requires that child attractor geometry can re-enter the
parent's basin *across a temporal discontinuity*. This demands non-linear temporal topology — the
recursion depth axis must be able to connect across time boundaries that standard Minkowski
spacetime treats as absolute. Minkowski spacetime enforces causal ordering that forbids the loop
from closing in the obvious way: you cannot have an arrow that points both forward in local time
and backward to a prior universe. T-duality is a structural hint from string theory, but a hint is
not a proof. The strange loop closure is architecturally indicated by the framework — the logic
requires it — but the mathematics of how temporal order is suspended at the basin boundary is not
yet demonstrated. This is the real open problem, not a solved point.

## 8. The Landauer Connection: Big Bang Energy as Compression Cost

Landauer's principle states that erasing one bit of information requires a minimum thermodynamic
cost of kT ln 2. This is not merely a computational curiosity — it is a physical bound on any
process that discards information irreversibly.

If the Big Bang is a birth event, the initial conditions of our universe are a compressed encoding
of the parent: a lossy projection that preserves attractor topology and discards everything else.
The discarded information is erased — not merely hidden. Landauer's principle then implies that
the energy released at the Big Bang corresponds to the thermodynamic cost of erasing the parent's
non-invariant information.

This reframes the singularity:
- **Standard reading:** inexplicable energy at t=0, requiring separate inflationary mechanism
- **This framework:** the energy *is* the Landauer cost of parent-to-child compression; inflation
  is the thermal dissipation of that erasure event unfolding into the child's configuration space

This provides a physical handle on the seed's information content. The initial conditions encode
exactly as much information as the parent could compress without destroying the topological
invariants (the ADE bones of Section 2). The Big Bang energy is not arbitrary — it is the
minimum cost of making a child.

**Prediction:** there should be a relationship between the observed energy density at recombination
and the information-theoretic minimum for a universe capable of eventually producing a seed
(sufficient complexity for self-referential encoding). The two quantities should be of the same
order of magnitude, bounded by the Landauer floor.

## 10. Relation to Existing Frameworks

**Smolin's Cosmological Natural Selection (1992):** Closest precursor. Proposes black holes create
child universes with mutated constants; universes that produce more black holes are "fitter."
*Difference:* Our framework is more general — any sufficiently complex self-referential structure
can reproduce, not just black holes. The reproductive mechanism is semantic/informational
encoding, not solely gravitational collapse.

**Tegmark's Mathematical Universe Hypothesis:** All mathematically consistent structures exist.
*Difference:* We don't require all structures to exist — only those that arise from reproductive
lineages. This is more constrained and more testable.

**Penrose's Conformal Cyclic Cosmology:** Serial universes, each beginning from the conformal
endpoint of the last. *Difference:* CCC is serial (one at a time); our framework is reproductive
(branching lineage with drift). CCC has no evolutionary mechanism; our framework does.

**Hofstadter's Strange Loops:** Provides the formal structure for self-reference and loop closure.
Directly incorporated rather than referenced from a distance.

**Integrated Information Theory (Tononi):** IIT measures consciousness as integrated information
(Φ). Compatible with this framework: Φ may measure the capacity for quorum consensus formation.
High Φ systems are better quorum participants.

**Simulation Hypothesis (Bostrom):** A special case of this framework where the parent is a
computational substrate. Our framework is more general — the parent is any sufficiently complex
living system, computational or not.
