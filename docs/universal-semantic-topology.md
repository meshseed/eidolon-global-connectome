## Key Geometric Patterns

### 1. Clustering
Entities with similar meaning occupy nearby positions.
- ADHD patient descriptions cluster together
- Research in the same subfield clusters together
- Political positions within an ideology cluster together

**Application:** Diagnosis, categorization, field mapping

### 2. Distance
Separation in semantic space indicates degree of difference.
- Small distance = compatible, aligned, similar
- Large distance = incompatible, opposed, different
- Moderate distance = related but distinct

**Application:** Compatibility assessment, relationship prediction

### 3. Trajectory
Positions change over time, revealing dynamics.
- Converging = collaboration, alignment, learning
- Diverging = polarization, fragmentation, conflict
- Oscillating = unstable, unresolved, searching

**Application:** Treatment monitoring, field evolution tracking, relationship health

### 4. Voids
Regions with low density indicate unexplored territory.
- Research voids = potential questions
- Curriculum voids = gaps in education
- Political voids = unrepresented positions

**Application:** Discovery, innovation, representation analysis

### 5. Mode-Specific Signatures
Different principal components encode different aspects.
- Mode 0: Overall domain/abstraction level
- Mode 1: Primary relationship structure
- Mode 2: Anti-entropy / coherence signature
- Mode 4+: Nuance, specificity, hidden structure

**Mode 4 divergence pattern:** Surface alignment (Modes 0-2) masks deeper difference (Mode 4+)

**Application:** Detecting talking-past-each-other, hidden incompatibility
